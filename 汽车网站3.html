Understanding Incoterms

We support various international trade terms to provide flexibility in our business relationships. Here are some commonly used terms:

FOB (Free On Board)

Seller delivers goods on board the vessel nominated by the buyer at the named port of shipment. Risk transfers when goods cross the ship's rail.

Seller loads goods on board
Buyer arranges main transport
Risk transfers at ship's rail

CIF (Cost, Insurance and Freight)

Seller delivers goods on board the vessel and pays for insurance and freight to the named port of destination. Risk transfers at ship's rail.

Seller pays freight and insurance
Insurance covers minimum cover
Risk transfers at ship's rail

DDP (Delivered Duty Paid)

Seller delivers goods to the buyer at the named place of destination, cleared for import and all duties paid. Maximum responsibility for seller.

Seller pays all costs to destination
Seller handles import clearance
Risk transfers at destination
